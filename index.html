Wireless Microphones
For Zoom participants to hear in-room speakers clearly
Plug the USB MIC receiver dongle into the laptop
Hold the button to power on each microphone unit
1
Ensure both wireless microphones are charged.
2
Power each mic on by holding down the button until it activates.
3
Plug the USB mic receiver dongle into the laptop.
4
In Zoom, open Settings → Audio.
5
In the Microphone dropdown, select Microphone (USBAudio1.0).
6
Click Test Microphone to confirm audio is being received before your meeting starts.
Zoom → Settings → Audio — select Microphone (USBAudio1.0)

Sending Video & Audio to the Room TV
HDMI connection to the display next to the podium and above the AV rack
HDMI input on the wall — plug in here from the laptop
Zoom → Settings → Audio → Speaker — select the NS-42E470A13 display output
1
Connect the HDMI cable from the laptop's HDMI output port to the HDMI input on the wall.
2
In Zoom, open Settings → Audio.
3
In the Speaker dropdown, select NS-42E470A13 (HD Audio Driver for Display Audio).
4
Click Test Speaker to confirm audio routes to the room TV before guests arrive.
4
Handheld Microphones & AV Rack
Shure wireless handhelds for in-room amplification
1
Pull the AV rack away from the wall to access the keys for the glass door.
2
Unlock the glass door and turn on the main power switch inside the rack.
3
Power on both Shure handheld microphones using the power button on each unit.
4
Check the indicator light on each mic:
🟢 Green = batteries OK
🔴 Red = replace batteries
Green LED = battery is good. Replace batteries if the light shows red.
